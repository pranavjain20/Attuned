# Session — Mar 16, 2026

Spent this session doing deep research and then updating the PRD with everything we learned.

Started by researching how music actually affects the autonomic nervous system. Went through published studies on tempo and heart rate variability, energy/loudness and arousal, acousticness and relaxation, instrumentalness and cognitive load, major vs minor key effects, and why familiar music works better than generic playlists. Found concrete numbers — like 60-80 BPM music inhibits sympathetic activity, baroreflex sensitivity is significantly greater at 60 BPM than 120 BPM, and arousal (energy) matters more for physiology than valence (positive/negative).

Then researched HRV analysis from sports science — learned that the field standard is to log-transform RMSSD before computing anything (LnRMSSD), that 7-day rolling averages beat single-day readings, and found concrete thresholds for when HRV decline is concerning (>=20% below 30-day average). Also found HRV coefficient of variation thresholds from Buchheit 2014.

Researched sleep architecture — normal deep sleep is 15-20% of total, REM is 20-25%. Found that 1.5 standard deviations below personal mean is the right threshold for flagging a real deficit. Also researched cumulative sleep debt — Van Dongen 2003 showed >5 hours over a week causes measurable cognitive impairment and people don't even realize it.

Looked into LLM music classification accuracy — found it varies a lot by property. BPM is 85-90% accurate for popular songs but only 30-40% for obscure ones. Valence is the hardest at 60-70%. Batch size matters — quality drops above 10 songs per request, so we're doing 5. Found known biases: popularity bias, genre stereotyping, Western music bias, mid-range valence clustering.

Researched the iso principle for playlist sequencing — music therapy says start near the listener's current state and gradually shift. Found that 2 songs is too abrupt for transitions, need at least 3. Derived a concrete algorithm: 10-15 BPM shift per song, 0.1-0.15 energy delta.

Wrote all of this up as docs/RESEARCH.md — 444 lines covering every finding with full citations, the neurological scoring formulas with weights, and target property ranges for all six states.
