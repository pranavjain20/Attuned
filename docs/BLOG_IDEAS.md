# Blog Ideas

## Post 1: The story of discovering that feeling != recovery

**Core narrative:**
- I wanted music that catered to what I actually felt
- I used to think what I felt = my WHOOP recovery score
- So I built Attuned on that assumption — recovery drives the playlist
- It wasn't working. The playlists didn't match how I felt
- That was the biggest learning: how I'm feeling is NOT the same as my WHOOP recovery
- Started researching why that disconnect happens
- Went deep into the science — ANS, how different physiological signals map to subjective experience
- Realized recovery is one number (mostly HRV-derived), but feeling is the full picture: sleep quality, sleep debt, trends, respiratory rate, skin temp, all interacting
- That research led to the continuous neurological profile — not as a technical feature, but as a realization about what wearables are missing

**The hook:** everyone with a wearable has had the moment where the number says one thing and their body says another.

## Post 2: The bigger thesis — wearables should capture feeling, not readiness

- Discovered through building Attuned that the entire wearable framing is wrong
- The industry optimizes for readiness, recovery, strain — performance metrics
- But what actually matters is feeling, and that's a different, richer thing
- The neurological profile concept is bigger than music playlists
- Research-backed: this isn't soft, it's grounded in ANS science

Post 1 earns the right to make Post 2's claim. The reader goes on the journey with me first.
