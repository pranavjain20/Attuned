WINNING STRATEGY: genre+spread
-------------------------------
Algorithm:
  1. Determine base weights by category:
     - Indian songs: formula_weight=0.7, direct_weight=0.3
     - English songs: formula_weight=0.3, direct_weight=0.7
  2. Adjust by LLM confidence (measured by score spread):
     - Spread > 0.4: boost direct by +0.15
     - Spread 0.25-0.4: boost direct by +0.05
     - Spread < 0.25: reduce direct by -0.10
  3. Clamp direct_weight to [0.1, 0.9]
  4. Blend: score = formula * fw + direct * dw
  5. Dominant bucket wins

Why it works:
  - Formula is strong for Indian songs (uses BPM, energy, acousticness well)
    but terrible for English (LLM gives wrong BPM for known tracks)
  - Direct LLM scoring is strong for English (GPT-4o-mini recognizes these songs)
    but weak for Indian (doesn't know obscure tracks well)
  - Spread-based confidence adjustment catches cases where the LLM is unsure
    (all scores near 0.5 → low spread → rely on formula instead)

Miss analysis (8 misses):
  5 impossible (both formula and direct predict wrong bucket)
  2 rescuable (direct was right but strategy leaned formula — Die For You, Namo Namo)
  1 near-miss (formula was right but blend shifted it — Hanuman Chalisa Lo-fi)

RECOMMENDATION
--------------
Implement genre+spread as the production blending strategy.

68% is a 42% relative improvement over formula-only (48%) and 21% relative
improvement over direct-only (56%). The remaining 8 misses are dominated by
structurally impossible cases (both sources wrong), not strategy failures.

The 2 rescuable misses (Die For You, Namo Namo) both have low LLM spread
(0.10 and 0.30) which correctly signals low confidence — the strategy is
working as designed, it just happens that direct was right despite low
confidence. Trying to rescue these would likely hurt other predictions.

Next steps to improve beyond 68%:
  - Better formula: fix BPM classification for English songs (half-time BPM issue)
  - Better direct scoring: fine-tune LLM prompt for Indian songs
  - Add Essentia audio analysis as third signal source
  - The 6 "impossible" songs need fundamentally better input data
