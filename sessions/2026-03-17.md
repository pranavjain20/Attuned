# Session — Mar 17, 2026

Spent this session building the engagement scoring system — computing a 0.0-1.0 score per song from behavioral signals so the matching engine knows which songs I actually love vs ones that just happened to autoplay.

Started with 5 signals: log-normalized play count, completion rate (how much of the song I listen to on average), active play rate (did I intentionally click it), skip rate, and recency. Got the basic implementation working with 19 tests, then ran the first staff tester + auditor cycle. Tester added 38 more tests and found a timezone conversion bug. Auditor flagged 6 issues. Fixed everything, 177 tests passing.

Ran on real data and immediately hit a big problem — 435 duplicate song groups. Spotify re-releases tracks with new URIs, so Namo Namo had 14 plays on one URI and 82 on another instead of 96 combined. Built a dedup module that finds exact (name, artist) matches, picks the best URI, reassigns all listening history, and merges metadata. 52 tests for dedup.

Then the real tuning started. Ride It and Excuses were showing up in the top 10 despite not listening to them in over a year — the recency signal was just checking the last-played date, so one stray play inflated it. Replaced it with recent_play_ratio (plays in last year / total plays). Ride It dropped from 0.83 to 0.47.

Discovered that my two Alexas (bedroom Echo Dot with 2,300+ plays, living room one showing as "not_applicable" with 2,100+ plays) were being penalized — the formula only counted app taps as intentional, but Alexa voice commands show up as "remote" and "playbtn." Shiv Kailash went from active=0.33 to 0.48 after fixing this.

Also found that 3 plays was too few for confident scoring — Paan Ki Dukaan ranked #20 with just 3 perfect plays. Raised the engagement threshold to 5+ plays. Set a separate classification threshold at 2+ plays (1,360 songs) since the LLM just needs to know the musical properties, not behavioral confidence.

Did a side-by-side comparison of recency weight at 0.20 vs 0.15 — went with 0.20 because 0.15 let Ride It and Excuses creep back into the top 25. Final weights: log_play 0.30, completion 0.25, recent_ratio 0.20, active 0.15, (1-skip) 0.10. 669 songs scored. Top 5: Bernie's Chalisa, Namo Namo, Boyfriend, Aayi Nai, Shiv Kailash. 232 tests across 10 files, two full rounds of staff tester + auditor.
